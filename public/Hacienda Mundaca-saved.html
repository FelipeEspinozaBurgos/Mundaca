The Heritage

A Legacy Built by Love & Adventure

Constructed in the late 19th century by the infamous Fermín Mundaca—trader, pirate, and architect—this hacienda was dedicated to a woman known as "La Trigueña." Today, it stands as a testament to history and the enduring beauty of Isla Mujeres.

Eco-Conscious Living

We don't just visit the land; we protect it. Our commitment to sustainability is woven into every brick and every tour.

Low-Impact Tours

Strict visitor limits and designated paths to ensure minimal disruption to the local ecosystem.

Community Support

80% of our staff are locals from Isla Mujeres, and we source all tour supplies from island artisans.

Solar & Water

The hacienda operates on 100% solar energy and uses a sophisticated rainwater harvesting system.
