Green Commitment

Our Focus on Sustainability

Hacienda Mundaca Eco-Tour stands as a self-sustaining eco-historical park that integrates five key green pillars to address Isla Mujeres' local environmental challenges while delivering a unique, responsible tourism experience.

Essential Practices

1.- Solar Energy for Trails and General Lighting

At Hacienda Mundaca, we harness solar energy to power pathways and general nighttime lighting through discreetly installed panels and motion-sensor LED lanterns. This approach preserves the historical integrity of the site while significantly reducing our carbon footprint and operational energy costs.

  • Zero-impact illumination that respects the nocturnal environment and historical structures.
  • Lower electricity consumption and operational independence from the grid.
  • Contribution to Isla Mujeres' transition toward renewable energy.
  • Enhanced visitor safety on evening tours without light pollution.

Solar powered roads.

2.- Water Reuse and Treatment System

Our rainwater harvesting system combined with greywater treatment provides irrigation water across the estate, directly alleviating the water scarcity challenges faced by Isla Mujeres. This closed-loop solution reduces demand on potable water resources and supports lush, restored gardens without straining local supplies.

  • Rainwater collection from roofs and surfaces for non-potable use.
  • Greywater filtration and reuse for landscaping and flushing.
  • Significant reduction in freshwater consumption per visitor.
  • Long-term resilience against drought and supply interruptions.

Water cycle: capture, treat and reuse.

3.- Based Sargassum Biofertilizer Production

We transform sargassum, a recurring challenge for Caribbean beaches, into a valuable resource. Collected from nearby coasts, the seaweed undergoes controlled composting to produce an organic biofertilizer that nourishes the gardens and green areas of the estate, closing the circle of waste and eliminating synthetic chemicals.

  • Reduces sargassum accumulation on Isla Mujeres beaches.
  • Enriches soil naturally, promoting biodiversity and plant health.
  • Eliminates reliance on imported chemical fertilizers.
  • Creates a circular economy model replicable across the island.

Sargassum is reborn.

4.- Dry Bathrooms / Ecological Bathrooms

Our dry ecological restrooms feature waste separation technology in harmonious, discrete modules that blend with the historical surroundings. They require no water for flushing, prevent groundwater contamination, and maintain impeccable hygiene standards.

  • Substantial water savings in a water-stressed destination.
  • Zero liquid discharge, protecting the island's fragile aquifer.
  • Odor-free, user-friendly design with easy maintenance.

Modern dry sanitation.

5.- Waste Management & Environmental Education

Separate waste bins, clear educational signage, short 5-minute talks, mini-workshops and our Responsible Visitor program empower all guests to participate. Environmental awareness is built into the experience, encouraging lasting behavior change.

  • Daily guided briefings on site sustainability practices.
  • Interactive mini-workshops during tours.
  • Incentives for low-waste participation.
  • Ongoing education for both tourists and local staff.

Small actions, island-wide impact.
